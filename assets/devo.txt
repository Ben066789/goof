Isaiah 30:15
“In repentance and rest is your salvation, in quietness and trust is your strength, but you would have none of it.”

This was spoken by the prophet Isaiah during a time when Israel was under pressure and uncertainty. They were trying to figure things out and secure themselves.

And God gave them a simple answer.
Their strength was not in doing more. It was in rest, quietness, and trust.

That is not usually how we think about strength.

We often connect strength with effort, energy, or intensity. Especially in a praise and worship team, it can feel like you always need to be on, focused, sharp, expressive, and consistent.

But this verse shows a different kind of strength.

Even as a drummer, you understand this. Strength is not just about hitting hard or doing more fills. Sometimes the most important parts are the controlled ones. The steady tempo. The space you allow. The discipline to not overplay.

If you fill every gap, the song gets messy.
But when you stay grounded and locked in, everything becomes clearer and stronger.

Life and ministry are the same.

God is not asking you to carry everything or prove yourself through constant effort. He is inviting you to a place where your strength comes from trusting Him, even when things feel uncertain.

So instead of always asking, “Am I doing enough?”
Ask, “Am I trusting God even while I serve?”

Because just like in music, where control and timing create impact,
in life, quiet trust is what builds real strength.

But the verse does not end there.

It continues with a line that shifts everything.
“but you would have none of it.”

That means God already showed them the source of their strength, but they rejected it. Instead of trusting Him, they chose to rely on their own plans and control.

And that is the tension for us too.

We can know that our strength comes from God
but still choose to overthink, force things, or depend on ourselves.

So the question is not just where strength comes from.
It is whether we will actually choose that way.

Because God is offering it.
The question is, will we accept it?
